Ensuring high-quality care

Making high-quality abortion care a priority—even in the world’s most remote regions

No matter where they live, women and girls should have access to the highest possible quality of abortion and contraceptive care. Working with partners, governments and colleagues, we provide training and support for providers and health facilities, with the goal of optimizing services and quality of care.

"Improving quality makes a real difference in women’s lives."

Tibebu Alemayehu, Ipas senior medical advisor

Support for providers

At many Ipas-supported health facilities around the world, newly-trained abortion providers receive individualized follow-up support and guidance through our clinical mentoring program, which pairs them with experienced clinicians.

The clinical mentoring I received will certainly improve patient care, as my mentor encouraged me to engage with the women, to counsel them and motivate them to come again for follow-up care.

A provider in India

Facility monitoring

Clinical experts visit Ipas training and clinical sites regularly to help providers and facility staff make quality improvement part of the routine, ongoing process providing abortion-related care. It’s not about checklists – it’s about identifying challenges and coming up with solutions to improve care.

Clinical guidance

Our Clinical Updates in Reproductive Health, updated annually, offer health providers and trainers the most up-to-date, evidence-based clinical recommendations on comprehensive abortion care.

Successful initiatives

In Nigeria, Nepal, Ghana and other countries, clinical and programmatic support provided by Ipas is making abortion services more available, accessible and acceptable to women—and providing them with the highest attainable standard of care.
