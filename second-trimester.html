Second-trimester care

Eliminating deaths and injuries from unsafe abortion cannot be achieved without expanding access to second-trimester abortion, which disproportionately affects the poor, the young and those experiencing violence. Globally, the majority of abortion-related deaths and injuries occur in the second trimester.

We work with health providers to help them gain the technical skills and knowledge needed to perform second-trimester services in accordance with the World Health Organization’s guidelines on safe abortion care. We also work with health systems to expand their capacity for providing second-trimester care.

Young women in particular are likely to need second-trimester services because they may not realize they are pregnant in the early stages or may be hiding the pregnancy out of fear and shame.

Dr. Alison Edelman, OB/GYN and Ipas senior clinical consultant
