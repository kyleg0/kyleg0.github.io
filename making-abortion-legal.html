Making abortion legal

When abortion is a crime, women suffer—along with their families and communities.

Laws that criminalize abortion presume that the threat of arrest or imprisonment will prevent women from having abortions. The reality is women will always need abortion. But criminal abortion laws do little to reduce the number of abortions—they just make abortion more likely to be unsafe. Ultimately, criminal abortion laws do grave harm to women’s health and hu­man rights and further stigmatize abortion, a safe and common medical procedure. Laws should not be used to scare, shame or imprison a woman for ending a pregnancy.

Ipas works in countries around the world to promote laws and policies that support sexual and reproductive rights, including the right to safe abortion. We partner with and support local advocacy groups working for law reform, educate communities and policymakers about the life-saving benefits of safe and legal abortion, and we collaborate with governments to ensure policies in the health and justice systems help women access abortion where it’s already legal.

Few people realize that women actually go to jail in countries where abortion is illegal. It's a connection people don't make.

Ipas Senior policy advisor

Nearly 1 in 4 women in Rwandan prisons are jailed on charges of illegal abortion
